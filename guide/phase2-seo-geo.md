# Executive summary

Your three directives translate into three engineering jobs:

1. **"We are an inner wellness platform, not journaling"** → an *entity-consistency* job. One definition, everywhere, in schema, on-page, and off-site, so Google and every LLM learn exactly one thing about GenMyo.
2. **"Rank wherever people search inner wellness"** → a *problem-space coverage* job. Own the felt-problem queries ("feeling disconnected," "how to reconnect with myself") that have real volume and real intent, all framed as inner wellness, all funneling to one place.
3. **"Fill the form → straight to WhatsApp"** → a *single-conversion-path* job. One primary action on the whole site: a short, warm form that ends in an instant WhatsApp handoff, instrumented end-to-end so you can see and fix the drop-off.

The site now renders (prior work). The task now is *conversion efficiency*. The math for your 1,000-users/month ambition: at a realistic 10% form-start rate × 70% form-completion × 75% WhatsApp-handoff = **~5% of sessions become WhatsApp users** → **~20,000 sessions/month**. Every point you win on form completion or handoff completion lowers the traffic you need. **The leverage is in the form and the handoff, not in more pages.**

Biggest risks to the ambition: (1) the form is friction disguised as a conversion step — every extra field cuts completion ~7–10%; (2) the WhatsApp handoff on desktop silently dies at the WhatsApp Web login wall; (3) "inner wellness" as pure positioning gives you nothing to rank on unless you build the problem-space around it.
